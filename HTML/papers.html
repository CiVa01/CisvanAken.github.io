Bachelor Thesis on Neuromorphic Computing

I wrote my thesis on Neuromorphic Computing, specifically on the energy efficiency of Neuromorphic Algorithms compared to traditional algorithms. Ultimately graded with an 8 average.

The scope of the project changed often throughout the project, as it was a highly complex and novel topic, that had not yet before been assigned to Bachelor students. I learned a lot about how to deal with ambiguity and uncertainty in research, as well as how to adapt to changing circumstances.

A machine Learning model to classify MEG data

For a course in Machine Learning we build a model that combines LSTM and CNN units to filter both temporal and spatial data, to classify MEG data across different tasks.

My main contributions were conceptually, where I used my previous expertise in neural networks to help design the architecture of the model, as well as practically, where I collaborated on implementing the model in PyTorch, before others would run the evaluation.

Mental Health Effects of Social Media

We wrote an ethical analysis of the use of Social Media and how much responsibility is with the company behind such systems, and how much is with the actual users.

This work was a highly collaborative effort, where I contributed to the research and writing of the paper, developing the framework we used to analyze the ethical aspects of the problem.
